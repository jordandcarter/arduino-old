This is supposed to be a 30 second snippet of what you do, so if you have to explain it on an elevator, you can do so before the person gets to their floor. After 8 years of practice, I think I&#39;ve got the SparkFun elevator speech down to about 11 minutes. Even after 11 minutes of explanation, the person asks &quot;So you sell iPods and stuff?"Yep. That&#39;s what we do here. Admittedly, us here, and you there, are on to something. But I don&#39;t think anyone quite knows what, and putting your finger on it is like poking fog.</p> <p> And if I&#39;ve learned anything in the past 8 years of running SparkFun, it&#39;s that word of mouth recommendations are huge. Whether it&#39;s new music recommendations from friends, or someone helping me out with making a new part function, I place a lot more weight on personal connections. Now if we extend this to SparkFun users, if I don&#39;t have a good elevator pitch, how can I hope to have you all explain SparkFun to your friends?</p> <p style="text-align: center;"> <a href="http://www.fistfuloftalent.com/2010/01/wanted-normal-employee.html" target="_blank"><img alt="http://sparkfun.com/tutorial/news/donimo_normal.jpg" src="http://sparkfun.com/tutorial/news/donimo_normal.jpg" /></a><br /> Image lifted from this interesting <a href="http://www.fistfuloftalent.com/2010/01/wanted-normal-employee.html" target="_blank">HR blog</a></p> <p> So what does SparkFun do? I think we sell shortcuts. If you need a barometric pressure sensor, you may come across the <a href="http://www.sparkfun.com/products/9694" target="_blank">BMP085</a> by Bosch. It looks like a great sensor, but it comes in a leadless SMD package. It may require some basic external components (decoupling caps, pull up resistors, etc.). Now let&#39;s look at the barriers to using this new technology:</p> <ul> <li> It requires a custom PCB and new footprint to be designed</li> <li> It requires soldering, most likely SMD</li> <li> It requires firmware of sorts to get the IC to function</li> </ul> <p> Now you could design the PCB, soldering the IC, and write some firmware. But what happens when things go wrong?</p> <ul> <li> Did I mess up the PCB? Did I get the footprint wrong?</li> <li> How good is my soldering? Did I bridge some connections? Do I feel comfortable with tight pitch components?</li> <li> Is my firmware working correctly?</li> </ul> <p> These potential pitfalls compound each other. We&#39;ve all been there! It&#39;s quite aggravating to spend days on a particular sub-project when I should be focusing on the bigger picture. So what does SparkFun do? We hit all those barriers for you:</p> <ul> <li> We&#39;ve been through many prototypes to get to a &#39;known good&#39; design</li> <li> We&#39;ve got the SMD tools and processes to solder whatever the new IC or technology may require</li> <li> We&#39;ve tested the board so that you know it works</li> <li> We write example skeleton code to get you headed in the right direction</li> <li> And for good measure we release the engineering files on most products. These contain &#39;known good&#39; footprints and basic functional schematics in case you want to spin your own PCB some day.</li> </ul> <p> The products we sell are projects themselves. Nearly anyone can create the BMP085 breakout board at home - we try hard to show you that you can do it yourself (see Beginning Embedded Electronics <a href="http://www.sparkfun.com/tutorials" target="_blank">tutorials</a>). We just save you piles of time - a shortcut.</p> <p> What else do we do? I realized long ago that if I needed something for a project, someone else probably did as well. I use an Xacto knife to <a href="http://www.sparkfun.com/tutorials/42" target="_blank">cut PCB traces</a>, so we started <a href="http://www.sparkfun.com/products/9200" target="_blank">selling one</a>. I found myself using a handful of different LEDs for projects, let&#39;s sell a kit of <a href="http://www.sparkfun.com/products/9881" target="_blank">common LEDs</a>. OMG - I hate making cables and finding mating connectors, let us <a href="http://www.sparkfun.com/products/9916" target="_blank">take care of that</a>. We build stuff just like you and if you hit a barrier, we&#39;ll try to help you over it.</p> <p> We&#39;ve also been called &#39;the DigiKey filter&#39;. For the beginner in electronics, <a href="http://www.digikey.com" target="_blank">DigiKey</a>, <a href="http://www.mouser.com" target="_blank">Mouser</a>, and the rest of the big catalog distributors can be very daunting to navigate. Try searching for an &#39;LED&#39; on any of these sites and you&#39;ll get the phone book. If you&#39;ve just made an LED blink for the first time, you probably don&#39;t know if you need &quot;T-1 3/4"or &quot;1206&quot;. What? We offer only a few LEDs - the ones that cover 90% of the applications out there. If you&#39;re wondering, <a href="http://www.sparkfun.com/products/9590" target="_blank"><em>this</em></a> is the LED you probably need. We don&#39;t carry every trimpot, we carry the trimpot <a href="http://www.sparkfun.com/products/9806" target="_blank">you probably need</a>. We enjoy getting beginners started, knowing quite well that someday you&#39;ll graduate and order directly from these much larger companies. We will never sell every resistor. We will never try to out-DigiKey DigiKey.</p> <p> Therein lies my problem. The above summary is long and merely scratches the surface. And if I can&#39;t summarize what SparkFun does, how can I expect anyone who uses SparkFun goodies to recommend us to their friends? I would like to give you a one liner like &#39;Sharing Ingenuity&#39; but I want more. So what do you tell friends you do?
